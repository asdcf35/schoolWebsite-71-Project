school achievements

Regional Science Bowl
Won first prize in Regional Science Bowl

MORGANTOWN, W.Va. – Suncrest Middle School claimed victory at the 30th annual West Virginia Regional Science Bowl, held February 5-6, 2021, in a virtual setting.One of 11 middle school teams participated in the competition, which is sponsored by the U.S. Department of Energy’s (DOE) National Energy Technology Laboratory (NETL). The West Virginia Science Bowl (WVSB) tested students’ knowledge of math and science topics. Kinsey Walker, the STEM education and outreach coordinator for the NETL West Virginia site said she was impressed with the level of competition this year.

Spelling Bee
Came first in Regional Spelling Bee

Pearl Zhang, an eighth-grade student at Monongalia County’s Suncrest Middle School, made it through 20 rounds lasting close to three hours to win the competition, which was held in FSU’s Wallman Hall. Zhang spelled the word “ephedrine” to win the competition, which included 54 students representing schools in 10 counties.

Campus Life

  • New Building

    Due to our students excelling in county as well regional competitions, more and more students are moving from their previous schools. Most of these students are 5th grader, as they are finished with their elementary schooling and can freely choose which school they want to go to. As such with these demands, we are proud to announce the new building called the Annex. The Annex is a building that was built for 6th graders. There are classes and the 6th grade tech room, where we can ask for our tech help. We hope you enjoy!

  • New Chromebooks

    We are using new chromebooks. These chromebooks give some benefits:

    1. Speed:
    Earlier we had slow chromebooks. While they weren't a big issue, they were a big issue once we used more powerful software. Now with the new chromebooks, the chromebooks are able to handle more powerful software, causing students to turn in their homework on time.

    2. Stability:
    Older chromebooks had less stable versions of ChromeOS due to using Debian Stretch. Now with the new chromebooks, we can upgrade to Debian Buster, which is the new version, and has stability features.

    3. Security:
    With the older chromebooks, students chromebooks were vulenerable to malicious attacks. While they weren't a big deal when taken home, but were a huge deal on school grounds, as hackers are able to go into the county schools WiFi and be able to control it.
